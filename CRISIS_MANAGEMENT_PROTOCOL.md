# Crisis Management Protocol

This Crisis Management Protocol defines the procedures System Of All Story follows when responding to emergencies, high‑risk incidents, misinformation events, security breaches, or reputational threats. The goal is to ensure rapid, coordinated, and transparent action while protecting editorial integrity and user trust.

---

## 1. Crisis Categories

A crisis includes any event that may:

- Threaten platform security  
- Compromise user data  
- Damage editorial credibility  
- Involve misinformation or factual errors  
- Trigger legal or regulatory exposure  
- Impact public safety  
- Involve advertiser misconduct  
- Create reputational risk  

Crises are classified into four categories:

### 1.1 Editorial Crisis
- Publication of inaccurate or misleading information  
- Unverified medical or financial claims  
- Mislabeling of sponsored content  
- Political neutrality concerns  

### 1.2 Security Crisis
- Unauthorized access  
- Data breach  
- Infrastructure compromise  
- API or integration vulnerabilities  

### 1.3 Legal & Compliance Crisis
- Regulatory inquiries  
- DMCA or copyright claims  
- Trademark misuse  
- Advertising violations (especially crypto)  

### 1.4 Reputational Crisis
- Public backlash  
- Viral misinformation  
- Misinterpretation of content  
- Third‑party association risks  

---

## 2. Crisis Response Team (CRT)

The following roles coordinate crisis response:

- **Editor‑in‑Chief** – Leads editorial crisis response  
- **Compliance Officer** – Handles legal and regulatory issues  
- **Technical Lead** – Manages security and infrastructure incidents  
- **Communications Lead** – Oversees public statements  
- **Advertising Lead** – Handles advertiser‑related crises  

Each crisis is assigned a lead based on category.

---

## 3. Immediate Response Procedure

When a crisis is detected:

### Step 1 — Acknowledge the Incident  
- Log the issue internally  
- Notify the Crisis Response Team  
- Freeze related content or systems if necessary  

### Step 2 — Assess Severity  
Determine if the incident is:
- Low impact  
- Moderate impact  
- High impact  
- Critical  

### Step 3 — Contain the Issue  
Actions may include:
- Temporarily removing content  
- Disabling affected systems  
- Blocking suspicious access  
- Pausing advertising campaigns  

### Step 4 — Begin Investigation  
- Identify root cause  
- Collect evidence  
- Document timeline  
- Interview involved team members  

---

## 4. Editorial Crisis Protocol

For editorial issues:

### 4.1 Immediate Actions
- Remove or temporarily unpublish affected content  
- Add a placeholder notice if necessary  
- Begin fact‑checking review  

### 4.2 Correction Workflow
- Verify facts  
- Issue correction or clarification  
- Update timestamps  
- Document the correction in the internal log  

### 4.3 Retraction Protocol
If content fails verification:
- Retract the article  
- Publish a retraction notice  
- Document the decision  

---

## 5. Security Crisis Protocol

### 5.1 Containment
- Disable compromised accounts  
- Revoke access tokens  
- Isolate affected systems  
- Activate incident response procedures  

### 5.2 Investigation
- Identify breach vector  
- Review logs  
- Assess data exposure  

### 5.3 Notification
If required by law:
- Notify affected users  
- Notify regulatory authorities  

### 5.4 Remediation
- Patch vulnerabilities  
- Strengthen access controls  
- Conduct post‑incident review  

---

## 6. Legal & Compliance Crisis Protocol

### 6.1 Immediate Actions
- Notify Compliance Officer  
- Freeze related content or advertising  
- Review applicable laws  

### 6.2 Documentation
- Record all communications  
- Preserve evidence  
- Prepare internal report  

### 6.3 Resolution
- Issue takedown if required  
- Update policies if needed  
- Respond to regulators or claimants  

---

## 7. Reputational Crisis Protocol

### 7.1 Monitoring
- Track social media and public sentiment  
- Identify misinformation or misinterpretation  

### 7.2 Response Strategy
- Publish clarification if needed  
- Avoid escalation  
- Maintain factual, neutral tone  

### 7.3 Post‑Crisis Review
- Evaluate communication effectiveness  
- Update editorial or communication guidelines  

---

## 8. Communication Guidelines

### 8.1 Internal Communication
- Use secure channels  
- Share verified information only  
- Maintain confidentiality  

### 8.2 External Communication
- Communications Lead issues all public statements  
- Tone must be factual, neutral, and transparent  
- Avoid speculation  
- Provide updates only when verified  

---

## 9. Post‑Crisis Review

After resolution:

- Conduct a full debrief  
- Document lessons learned  
- Update policies and procedures  
- Implement preventive measures  
- Review team performance  

---

## 10. Record Keeping

All crises must be logged with:
- Date and time  
- Category  
- Description  
- Actions taken  
- Final resolution  
- Responsible parties  

Records are retained for compliance and audit purposes.
